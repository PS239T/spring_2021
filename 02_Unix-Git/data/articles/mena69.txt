"  Behzad Naziri holding a picture of his sister, who he said was killed by authorities in Iran. With him was Kazem Radjavi of the People's Mujahedeen, an Iranian opposition group operating out of Paris. They spoke in New York and appealed to the United Nations to adopt a resolution condemning Iranian human rights abuses. Mr. Naziri said he was arrested on June 25, 1982, for ''affiliation with the foreign press,'' while working for Agence France-Presse. ''I experienced myself the reality of persistent torture,'' he said, describing daily floggings that exposed bone and being forced to stand without food. (NYT/Don Hogan Charles)"
